Why is a Picture Worth a 1000 Words?

I don't think with words, I think with images which I can shape and visualize any way I like. Words I translate into images. To me, pictures transmit information in many forms almost instantly. Words do the same thing but it takes a lot longer. Pictures grab my attention, words do not.

20 Thought Provoking Questions to Ask a Client

    1. What is the impression you would like the designs to convey?
    2. What is your company mission?
    3. What are your strategic goals covering financial, business development, technological and operational?
    4. What is the company strategy for achieving those goals?
    5. What are your products and services? Are they the world's best? If yes, how?
    6. What is their positioning?
    7. Do you have any patents?
    8. Does your company have a brand name and image?
    9. What are your distribution channels?
    10. What are your business partnerships and relationships? What do they provide?
    11. Who are your customers? What do they do? What do you provide to them?
    12. Why do your customers choose your products and/or services?
    13. What do your customers tell you needs to be improved and/or implemented?
    14. Who are your major competitors? What do they do better, what do they do worse?
    15. When and by whom was your company formed?
    16. Where is your company's home office?
    17. Does your company have an international presence? If yes, where and what kind of presence?
    18. How many employees in the US? How many worldwide? How many by country?
    19. Does your company already have a web site? If yes, what are it's goals?
    20. Does your company do business on the web? If yes, what business?
    21. Does your company have an Intranet? If yes, what are it's goals?

Internet Engineering

The Internet is a vast collection of inter-connected networks. Internet Engineering is the design, building and improvement of integrated hardware and software components that comprise the Internet, how these components and layers of components relate to each other, how they perform the tasks required of the Internet structure and of the components that use the Internet.
