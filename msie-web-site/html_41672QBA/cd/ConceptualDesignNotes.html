Expert Review

An expert is someone who is not involved. An outside perspective. Everyone will find out whether he is an expert the moment he opens his mouth. Often used to examine a product etc.

A review is often used as justification, to show that something needs to be done. It reveals best practices, and shows how the current practices compare. If you are already in the top 50%, then probably nothing needs to be done. If you are in the bottom 20% a site redesign could have a significant impact on the business.

The 3 clicks rule is a myth. 5 Clicks are better if the user instinctively knows what to click. The user will find his quarry much faster and be happier.

Card Layout Exercise

The 5 colleges, as an example. Note the missions statement, a strategy declaration. On a board, setup the following sections:

  • Users
  • Goals
  • Problems
  • Solutions

Allow everyone to write down what they want. This way everyone gets heard. Don't have to deal with difficult people. Don't need to consider everything, thus can avoid unreasonable people.

Users have diferent needs. Break them down into groups (granularity).

Card Sort

We all took ten cards and wrote on them different pages for the web site. For example, faculty, course info, etc. We gathered the cards and placed duplicates together. Thus, we were left with page names for our site. Then we place the cards in a heirachy. Where a link needs to reference another card, a link mark is made on the card. The end result is a site map.
