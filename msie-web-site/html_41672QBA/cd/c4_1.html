Assignment

 

List 3 possible ideas for what you would like to do for your final design project. You should put these in order based on your interest (favorite first). Each project should have a short 1-2 sentence description of what you'd like to do, and then one sentence saying WHY you want to do that project. In chat next week we will go over all of your choices and finalize your decisions. Everyone must have a final site chosen and approved by either me or Jen by next Friday Oct 31st.

Here are some thoughts to keep in mind when picking a project:

  • Pick something that you can have fun with, and use it as a welcome break, or pick something that really pushes you where you need to go.
  • Pick something that you don't know anything about, and learn something new, or pick something that you know pretty well, which you'll be able to take to a new level of mastery.
  • Pick something that ties in with another class to kill two birds with one stone, or pick something that is very different from your other classes, to give you a break.
  • Work on a potential capstone idea to flush it out a little more and see if you really want to do that for a capstone, or to get a head start on your capstone, or maybe do something really different that you won't have time to do once you start on your capstone.
  • You have 7 weeks to complete this project, so be sure to pick a project that you can get a good chunk of work done on in this timeframe.
  • You shouldn't have a “client” for your site. If at the end of 7 weeks you have something you want to show to someone, great, but having a “client” can mean a lot of meetings, and limitations that you shouldn't let yourself get bogged down with.
